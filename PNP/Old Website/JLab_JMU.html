JLab at JMU: Halls B and C

Hall B: PMT Testing

At JMU, our role involving JLab's Hall B is to receive, assemle bases, test, label, and package the photomultiplier tubes that will be used in the CLAS-12 Hall B detector upgrade. We are assembling bases by soldering and testing the PMTs for Dark Current and gain. Our test mechanism for DC testing is a black box that we have constructed that allows no light into the PMTs, so that we can see the amount of current, literally "in the dark" that is coming into the PMTs. This allows us to provide JLab with information for each specific PMT so that they know what behavior to expect for any given PMT.

Hall C: Time Of Flight (TOF) System

The Time of Flight System (or TOF, for short) in the Hall C detector is the equipment that measures the velocity of an incoming particle. The particle will fly through two scintillator sheets that detect it, and measure its position and the time it takes to travel between the sheets. At JMU, our job is to glue PMTs to the two ends of scintillator bars that we received so that the measurements can be taken. We are constructing sliders with which we can accurately insert the PMTs into the scintillator holders, and then inserting them while gluing them in place. The two sheets of scintillator/PMT will be placed roughly 1m apart in the same plane, but in perpendicular directions. (x-y orthogonality) Once finished, the scintillator/PMT bars will be sent to JLAB in storage boxes which we have also designed and built from scratch. The storage boxes will be made of wood with a soft foam padding to protect the finished scintillator/PMT bars. All of these efforts will allow JLAB to install the TOF system in 2013, with an expected experiment starting date of 2014.
